Research Question

Are AI-generated questions good enough to use in the classroom?

Specifically, can GenAI reliably generate classroom-ready MCQs when guided by explicit pedagogical guardrails?

How We Evaluated Effectiveness

A question is classroom-ready when it passes both quality and relevance.

Generation Was Guided by a Carefully Designed Prompt

Quality guardrails
Well-built
  • One concept per question
  • Simple, plain language
  • Technically correct answers
  • Parallel answer choices
  • Avoid absolute terms
Relevance guardrails
On-target
  • Focus on important concepts
  • Emphasize core understanding
  • Avoid trivial edge cases

Prompt design was intentionally aligned with assessment best practices.

Key Takeaways

01
Most AI-generated questions were already usable with minimal review.
02
Refine quality was mainly small wording and option-balance edits.
03
Targeted instructor review can fix many issues without rewriting entire items.
04
The strongest workflow is AI draft generation plus lightweight human tuning.
In one sentence
GenAI works best as a drafting partner: instructors can preserve most of the item and apply small, high-impact edits during review.
